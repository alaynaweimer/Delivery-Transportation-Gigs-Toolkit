üìö Tax Deduction Guide for Gig Drivers

As a self-employed gig worker, you owe:

  • Self-employment tax: 15.3% (Social Security + Medicare)
  • Income tax: 10-15% (federal, depending on bracket)
  • Total: 25-30% of net income

Standard Mileage Deduction (Easiest):

Deduct $0.67/mile for 2024. This covers gas, maintenance, depreciation, insurance. Track every business mile!

Example: Drive 15,000 miles = $10,050 deduction. At 25% tax rate = $2,512 tax savings.

Other Deductible Expenses:

  • Phone/data plan (prorated for business use %)
  • Car washes, cleaning supplies
  • Hot bags, phone mounts, dash cams
  • Parking, tolls, fees
  • Roadside assistance membership

Quarterly Tax Payments:

Pay estimated taxes quarterly (April, June, Sept, Jan) to avoid penalties. Set aside 25-30% of net earnings.
