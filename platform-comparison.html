üìö Platform Comparison Guide

Not all gig platforms are created equal. Each has different pay structures, tip cultures, delivery distances, and customer bases. What works best in one market might not work in another.

üçï Food Delivery Platforms

DoorDash: Largest market share, most orders, but often lower base pay. Tips can be good. Best for: High-volume markets.

UberEats: Second-largest, higher base pay than DoorDash in some markets. Better surge pricing. Best for: Urban areas.

GrubHub: Older platform, declining market share, but often higher pay per order. Best for: Markets where it's still popular.

üõí Grocery Delivery

Instacart: Grocery shopping + delivery. Higher pay per order but more time-consuming. Tips can be excellent. Best for: Patient drivers who don't mind shopping.

üì¶ Package Delivery

Amazon Flex: Package delivery with guaranteed hourly rate ($18-25/hr). Fixed blocks, less flexibility. Best for: Drivers who want guaranteed pay.

üöó Rideshare

Uber/Lyft: Passenger transport. Higher earnings potential but vehicle wear from passengers, insurance requirements. Best for: Social drivers with newer, clean vehicles.

üí° Multi-App Strategy

Top earners run 2-3 apps simultaneously. Accept the best orders from whichever app pings first. This minimizes downtime between deliveries.
